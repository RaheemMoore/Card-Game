# Archetype Weapon and Companion Upgrade Paths

**Status:** Draft companion reference for approval  
**Project:** Card Game  
**Companion to:** `Archetype_Weapon_and_Companion_Reference.md`  
**Scope:** Three-tier visual and fantasy progression for every approved weapon family and companion/unit type

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how each approved weapon, bonded beast, undead servant, blood servant, controlled machine, and autonomous unit can visibly progress through three power tiers.

The paths are designed primarily for **character generation, card art direction, prompt construction, and visual progression**. They do not assign combat statistics or guarantee mechanics.

### Canonical tier relationship

| Upgrade tier | Card rank alignment | Visual goal |
|---|---|---|
| Tier I | Foundation | Functional identity, readable silhouette, restrained power |
| Tier II | Forged | Clear improvement, active power source, specialized details |
| Tier III | Ascended | Mythic authority, strong radiance or environmental effect, preserved base identity |

A Tier III object should look unmistakably more powerful than Tier I, but it must still be recognizable as the **same weapon family or companion type**.

### Shared upgrade signals

- Improved materials and construction
- More confident or specialized silhouette
- Active internal power source
- Runes, memories, blood, souls, lunar energy, living growth, reactor light, or sacred radiance
- Stronger response to the wielder or partner
- Controlled environmental influence
- Evidence of history, experience, repairs, vows, or self-modification

Avoid using size, spikes, glow, and floating debris as the only signals of power. Those are seasoning, not dinner.

**Type:** Longsword or greatsword  
**Core role:** A repaired heirloom carrying the history of previous bearers

# 4. Prompt Construction Pattern

Use the following order when generating an upgraded asset:

`Base Weapon or Companion Identity + Tier Construction + Power Source + Active Visual Effect + Relationship Cue + Archetype Motifs + Environment Interaction + Negative Prompt Rules`

### Example: Necromancer Soul Lantern

- **Tier I:** iron funeral lantern, empty interior, faint spirit traces visible in the glass, practical ritual tool, restrained cold light
- **Tier II:** one named or contracted soul burns as a pale flame, face occasionally visible in the glass, stronger runes, spirit-light spilling onto the wielder
- **Tier III:** several individually identifiable souls orbit inside and around the lantern like a constellation, the lantern radiates deathly authority, nearby shadows lean toward it, no anonymous screaming-soul mass

### Example: Mech Pilot Arc Blade

- **Tier I:** replaceable field blade powered by a visible cable and compact emitter
- **Tier II:** stabilized energy edge with heat vents, pilot-linked output control, and active diagnostic lights
- **Tier III:** core-synchronized blade that changes length and shape under direct command, radiating contained reactor power and leaving a precise energy wake

### Example: Beastmaster Guardian Beast

- **Tier I:** naturally formidable animal wearing practical protective gear and one matching bond mark
- **Tier II:** armor and movement are customized through shared experience; the Beastmaster and beast display synchronized posture and aura
- **Tier III:** ancestral or elemental potential awakens, but the creature remains a recognizable individual and willing partner

# 5. Visual Validation Checklist

Before approving an upgrade path or generated image, verify:

- The tier increase is visible at card size.
- The original weapon or companion type remains recognizable.
- The power source has a clear narrative explanation.
- Tier II is meaningfully stronger than Tier I, not merely cleaner.
- Tier III is mythic without becoming visually incoherent.
- The effect language matches the archetype.
- Companion autonomy and relationship rules remain intact.
- The upgrade does not accidentally establish unapproved combat mechanics.
- The character remains the primary focal point.
- The item can still fit the current Figma card composition.
